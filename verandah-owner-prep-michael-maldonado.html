🌿 Exterior First Impressions

Curb appeal sets the emotional tone before a tenant ever steps inside. These are the non-negotiables.

  • Lawn & LandscapingMowed, edged, trimmed — beds should be clean and mulched. Overgrowth signals neglect immediately.
  • Driveway & WalkwaysPressure wash any oil stains, mildew, or buildup. Cracks should be addressed if significant.
  • Front Door & EntryPaint or clean the front door. Replace or polish hardware. Add a simple, clean welcome mat.
  • Exterior Paint / SidingLook for peeling, fading, or mildew streaks. A pressure wash often resolves this without repainting.
  • Gutters & Roof VisibilityClear any visible debris. Tenants and inspectors notice sagging gutters and deferred roof maintenance.
  • LightingReplace burned-out bulbs at entry, garage, and pathway lighting. Warm light photographs beautifully.
  • Garage DoorTest operation, clean panels, replace weatherstripping if worn. A surprisingly high-impact item.
  • Fence & GatesSecure latches, repaint if faded, clear any vines or damage. Especially important for pet-friendly positioning.
🔧 Interior Systems — Safety & Function First

Before aesthetics, everything must work. These are verified by our team at move-in — and tenants notice failures within the first 48 hours.

  • HVACReplace filters, schedule a service if not done in the last 12 months, confirm thermostat functions correctly.
  • PlumbingCheck under all sinks, look for drips, test water pressure, ensure all drains flow freely.
  • ElectricalTest all outlets and switches. Ensure GFCI outlets are present in bathrooms and kitchen.
  • Smoke & CO DetectorsReplace batteries or units as needed. Florida law requires functioning detectors — non-negotiable.
  • Water HeaterNote the age and look for rust or sediment. Units over 10 years should be discussed before listing.
  • AppliancesTest each one. Confirm all included appliances work — dishwasher, range, refrigerator, microwave, washer/dryer if applicable.
  • Doors & LocksAll exterior doors must lock securely. Interior doors should latch properly. Keys should be accounted for.
✨ Interior Presentation — What Tenants Fall For

Clean, fresh, and neutral. You don't need to renovate — you need to present. These items move the needle most.

  • Deep CleanProfessional cleaning throughout — inside cabinets, appliances, baseboards, ceiling fans. Your most important dollar spent.
  • Walls & PaintPatch nail holes, touch up scuffs. If walls are heavily marked or dated, neutral paint is one of the highest-return investments.
  • FlooringSteam clean carpet. Refinish or clean hard floors. Address any trip hazards or lifting seams.
  • KitchenClean cabinet faces, replace broken hardware. Grout and caulk around sink should be fresh and bright.
  • BathroomsRe-caulk tub/shower if discolored. Replace toilet seats if worn. Clean or replace shower curtain rods.
  • Lighting & BulbsReplace all burned-out bulbs. Match color temperature throughout — warm white (2700K–3000K) photographs best.
  • Window TreatmentsClean or replace blinds that are broken or badly bent. Curtains should be washed or removed.
  • OdorsA silent deal-killer. Address pet, mildew, or smoke odor at the source — not with fragrance cover-up.
  • Personal Items & ClutterAll personal belongings and excess items must be fully removed before photos or showings.
